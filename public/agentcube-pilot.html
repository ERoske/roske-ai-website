Pilot Starters

Pick one. Prove it. Expand later.

These are the three most common pilot use cases. Each is narrow enough to finish in 30 days and valuable enough to justify the full rollout afterwards. You can also propose your own.

Variance Analysis on Demand

Your agent compares any actuals against any plan for any entity, and returns the top drivers of the variance. Common starting point for teams running Planning and Essbase.

Outcome: same-day variance answers instead of multi-day Smart View digs.

Close Status & Blocker Tracking

Your Controller asks where the close stands; your agent pulls entity status from FCC, identifies blockers, and lists the owners who need to act. Ideal for teams running FCC.

Outcome: real-time close visibility without chasing preparers.

Driver-Based Forecast Sandbox

Your FP&A lead adjusts a driver and gets back a projected forecast. Your agent writes to a working scenario in Planning, without touching the official model.

Outcome: scenario modeling in minutes instead of days.

Pilot Questions

The things teams ask before signing the pilot scope.

Can we run the pilot against production data?
We strongly recommend against it. Pilots should run in test or sandbox environments with representative data. Production rollouts come later, after security review and change management. Your data is safe either way, but pilot scope is cleaner in test.
Who runs the technical work?
Caprus engineering does the installation, provider integration, and agent configuration. Your team provides access, picks the use case, and tests. If you have a certified partner firm on retainer, they can do the implementation work instead, and we support.
What AI model should we use for the pilot?
Whatever your organization already has access to. If you're standardized on Claude, we use Claude. If you have an Azure OpenAI tenant, we use that. If you've deployed a model on-prem, we use that. The pilot is about proving the integration, not picking a model.
What if our Oracle environment is custom or heavily modified?
Most Oracle EPM deployments have some customization; that's normal. The pilot surfaces what works out of the box and what needs configuration. If your environment needs custom provider extensions, we'll tell you in week 1 and adjust scope accordingly.
Can we involve our Oracle partner or systems integrator?
Yes, and we encourage it. Bringing your existing EPM partner into the pilot makes the rollout path smoother; they learn AgentCube while we're still in scope. Certified Caprus partners get full access during pilots.
What if the pilot exposes a problem with our Oracle setup, not AgentCube?
Happens occasionally. We document what we find and include it in the readout. Sometimes the right next step is a pause to clean up upstream before rolling out AgentCube. That's a real outcome, and we'd rather surface it in week 4 than bury it.
Is the pilot fee negotiable?
The pilot fee is fixed because the scope is fixed. If the scope changes (additional provider, second use case, extended timeline), pricing adjusts transparently. We don't negotiate on the base fee because we're not optimizing for pilot revenue, we're optimizing for a clean path to the full license.
