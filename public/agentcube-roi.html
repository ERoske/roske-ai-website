What's Not in the Number

The calculator is conservative on purpose.

We'd rather undersell in the calculator and overdeliver in the pilot than the other way around. The projected annual value above is hard-dollar reclaimed time and close compression. Several real benefits are left out because they're harder to put a clean number on.

Faster decisions

Your CFO asks a question and gets an answer before the meeting ends. The downstream decision is better because it's grounded in data, not gut. That's real value. We can't put it in a spreadsheet.

Fewer errors

VLOOKUP-stitched spreadsheets introduce errors. Structured queries against the source of truth don't. The cost of one bad number in a board deck is often larger than the entire license.

Retention of senior staff

Your senior analysts did not take the job to build VLOOKUPs. When AgentCube takes the grunt work, the job becomes strategic again. Senior FP&A turnover gets expensive; this is a lever on it.

Board narrative on AI

"What's our AI story?" is asked of every CFO these days. Having a concrete, deployed, Oracle-native answer to that question has board-level political value, separate from the operating benefit.
