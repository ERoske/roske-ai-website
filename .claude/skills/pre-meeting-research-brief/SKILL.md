# Pre-Meeting Research Brief

Research attendees and generate strategic prep notes. Time to create: 20-30 minutes.

## Purpose

Walk into important meetings informed and prepared. Research attendees, company context, and generate talking points, questions, and conversation starters.

**Be extremely concise. Sacrifice grammar for the sake of concision.**

## Input Requirements

1. **Meeting Details**: Purpose, date, format
2. **Attendees**: Names, titles, companies
3. **Context** (optional): How you connected, desired outcome

## Core Structure

### Meeting Overview
- Purpose, objectives, success criteria

### Attendee Profiles
For each key attendee:
- Background (role, career, experience)
- Recent activity (posts, articles, speaking)
- Mutual connections

### Company Context
- Overview, recent news, strategic direction
- Why they need you

### Talking Points & Questions
- Discovery questions
- Strategic questions
- Conversation starters

### Meeting Approach
- Recommended tone
- Structure suggestion
- Desired outcome

## Optional Sections

### Red Flags to Watch
- Signs this isn't a good fit
- Unrealistic expectations

### Follow-Up Plan
- What to send after meeting
- Timeline for next steps

## Talking Points & Questions

### Discovery Questions (10-15 min)
"Walk me through your current FP&A process. What's working? What's frustrating?"
"How long does monthly close take? Where do you spend the most time?"
"When you think about scaling to $100M ARR, what keeps you up at night from a finance ops perspective?"

### Qualification Questions
"Have you allocated budget for this, or would it need approval?"
"What's your timeline? Is there a deadline driving this?"
"Who else would be involved in a decision like this?"

### Strategic Questions
"Most CFOs I talk to struggle with the ROI case for AI in finance. How are you thinking about measuring success?"
"You're in HR tech where there's lots of AI hype. How do you cut through noise to find tools that actually work?"

### Conversation Starters
**Opening**: "Thanks for making time, Sarah. Rather than me talking at you about what we do, I'd love to understand your current situation first."

**Mutual Connection**: "Jennifer Martinez mentioned you two worked together at SalesHub. She's been great to work with on their FP&A transformation."

**Compliment**: "I read your CFO Magazine quote about finance transformationâ€”you nailed the tension between moving fast and maintaining control."

## Meeting Approach

**Recommended Tone**: Peer consultant (not selling, collaborating)

**Structure** (30min):
- 0-5min: Rapport, set agenda
- 5-20min: Discovery (their situation, pain points)
- 20-27min: Brief overview + tailored value
- 27-30min: Next steps

**Desired Outcome**:
- Clear understanding of needs
- Budget/timeline confirmed
- Next meeting scheduled (with CFO if possible)

---

## Red Flags

**Budget**:
- "We're just exploring" (no urgency)
- "No budget allocated yet" (not ready)

**Authority**:
- "I'll need to run this by several people" (no decision power)

**Need**:
- "Things are working okay, just want to be more efficient" (nice-to-have, not must-have)

---

## Follow-Up Plan

**Within 1 Hour**:
Email thanking her, proposing next steps
- Brief proposal outline
- Schedule 45min with her + Michael (CFO)
- Timeline: Start in Nov/Dec

**If Positive**:
Prepare proposal presentation

**If Needs Time**:
Follow up 3-5 days later
